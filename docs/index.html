Personal Information

My name is Anne Kruijt, and I am a Dutch postgraduate student at the University of Verona in the Department of Linguistics. For my PhD project, I work on the case systems of the Alto-Adige region, in a Digital Humanities framework, in the spirit of which I am trying my hand on learning to build my own webpage through HTML5, CSS, and Javascript. For this reason this website might look a makeshift, as it is growing along my learning curve, and therefore it includes some crazy colours schemes, unnecessary tables, and loads of unneeded html syntax. It is also entirely possible it will not load or fit on a mobile device at all.

Where to find me

During the week days I can usually be found in the PhD-room (Dottorandi) on the second floor of the Palazzo Lingue, which is still quite sparse at the moment, but which is slowly being improved by a kettle, some plants, and, in the future, a splash of colour on the walls. In the weekends, I try to go out into the nature/countryside/mountains as much as possible, if the weather permits (which it usually does here). If not, I also enjoy wandering through nearly empty museums while being stalked by bored supposts.
