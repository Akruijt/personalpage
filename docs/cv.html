CV

Education
2018-present PhD, Linguistics, Digital Humanities, Program of Excellence, University of Verona, Italy. Project on description and analysis of the case systems of the Alto-Adige/Südtirol region, through digital platform VinKo.
2015-2017 Research Master Linguistics, cum laude, Leiden University, The Netherlands. Thesis: The functions and semantics of the ablative case clitic of Iraqw, a language of Tanzania.
2012-2015 Bachelor Linguistics, track Descriptive Linguistics, Leiden University, The Netherlands. Thesis: Predicative possession in the Pacific Northwest language area.
Work Experience
2017-2018 Linguistic Consultant, Productive Playhouse. Providing linguistic support in Dutch and working on several linguistic tasks, such as providing phonemic transcriptions for words, text normalization and verbalization, and evaluation of acoustic data.
2016-2017 Intern in Iraqw research group, supervised by Leiden University, Kwermusl, Tanzania. Set-up of community-based, collaborative and sustainable research group for the Iraqw language, teaching basic computer skills to local researcher, devising advisory report on future course of the project.
2016-2016 Research Assistant, University of British Columbia, Vancouver, Canada Collaborative project with the Heiltsuk community, digitization of lexicographic data, and creation of a syllabus on lexicography of indigenous and endangered languages.
2015-2015 Intern, in ‘Mixed languages’ project, Meertens Instituut, Amsterdam, The Netherlands. Transcribing and analysing audio file of Cité Duits (Dutch miners’ language variety) on morphosyntactic level, independent research.
2009-2016 Various part-time jobs Store employee, waitress, warehouse employee.
